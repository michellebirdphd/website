Starting small, shipping early

It is easy to wait for a personal website to feel finished before it goes live. A better approach is usually to start with something compact, readable, and honest about where the work is today.

A small site creates momentum. A landing page gives people context. A blog gives you a place to think in public. Project pages make it easier to explain what you are building without repeating the same background in every conversation.

This version of the site is intentionally simple: static pages, lightweight styling, and a deployment flow that does not require much maintenance. That means the real energy can go into writing and documenting the work rather than debugging an overly complicated setup.

The goal is not perfection. The goal is to make publishing so easy that future updates feel natural.
