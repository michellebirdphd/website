Developing Pedagogical Confidence in PE for Pupils with ADHD

This working paper explores how PE teachers build pedagogical confidence when teaching pupils with ADHD in mainstream school settings. The central focus is on practical teaching moves that can be implemented without extensive additional resources.

The project examines confidence as a developmental process rather than a fixed trait. Teachers often become more effective through iterative planning, collaborative reflection, and context-sensitive adaptation.

Current drafting themes include:

  • Instructional clarity and structured lesson transitions
  • Task design that supports attention and autonomy
  • Assessment approaches that recognize diverse progress
  • School-level conditions that sustain inclusive practice

Next steps include refining the conceptual framework and integrating practitioner-informed examples to strengthen relevance for PE teachers, teacher educators, and school leaders.
